\opening{Dear Editor,}

I am pleased to submit our manuscript entitled ``Symmetry-Aware Attention Networks for Student Engagement Detection: A Lightweight Temporal Approach'' for consideration for publication in \textit{Symmetry}.

This paper proposes a symmetry-aware framework for student engagement detection from facial expressions, with three key contributions that connect to the scope of \textit{Symmetry}:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Symmetry-aware attention}: We introduce a multi-scale Channel-Spatial Attention Module (CSAM) that captures both symmetric (engaged) and asymmetric (disengaged) facial patterns through parallel 3×3, 5×5, and 7×7 convolutions with adaptive fusion.
    
    \item \textbf{Symmetry-breaking detection}: We frame engagement detection as identifying symmetry-breaking phenomena in facial behavior, where engaged states exhibit symmetric muscle activation while disengaged states manifest asymmetric deviations.
    
    \item \textbf{Temporal symmetry analysis}: We introduce a BiLSTM-based temporal aggregation module that captures temporal symmetry/asymmetry patterns in video sequences, achieving 94.8\% video-level accuracy on DAiSEE.
\end{enumerate}

Key results:
\begin{itemize}
    \item FER2013: 70.5\% accuracy (5-fold CV, 1.26M parameters)
    \item CK+ cross-dataset: 71.9\% accuracy
    \item DAiSEE: 94.8\% video-level accuracy (vs 61.2\% frame-level)
\end{itemize}

This work is directly relevant to \textit{Symmetry} as it addresses symmetry and asymmetry patterns in facial expressions for engagement detection, and proposes symmetry-aware attention mechanisms for efficient edge deployment.

Both authors have read and approved the manuscript. We declare no conflicts of interest.

I look forward to your decision.

\closing{Sincerely,}
